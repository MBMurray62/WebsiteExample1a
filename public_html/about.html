About Aingeala

Early years

I grew up in an Irish-speaking family in an atmosphere of Irish language, music and culture in the wider context of the English speaking capital city of Ireland, Dublin. From the age of 5, I received an excellent all-round classical music education in the Royal Irish Academy of Music, Dublin and a ll the performing experience I had as a child made me hopeful of a future career as a classical violinist.

However, on completion of my studies, I was asked to join rock band In Tua Nua in 1985, and my musical interests took a new turn. For the first time in my life, I discovered the challenges but also the sheer fun of improvising. With In Tua Nua, I performed on the two albums released by the band with Virgin Records, ‘Vaudeville’ (1986) and ‘The Long Acre’ (1987). Life was full with countless gigs around Ireland, Britain, Europe and the USA including significant performances on ‘Self Aid’ in 1986, supporting Simple Minds in 1986, and U2 on their Joshua Tree European tour in 1987.

After a thoroughly rock ‘n roll three years, my yearning for classical playing led me to return to studying, and I kick-started my freelance classical career by graduating from Trinity College Dublin four years later with an honours degree in Music.
